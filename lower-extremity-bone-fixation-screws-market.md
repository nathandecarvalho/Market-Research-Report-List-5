<p><p>The lower extremity bone fixation screws market includes three main types: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable screws. Stainless steel screws are durable and provide strong fixation but may lead to metal-related complications. Titanium screws are lightweight, biocompatible, and corrosion-resistant, making them suitable for sensitive patients. Bioabsorbable screws, made from materials that gradually dissolve in the body, reduce the need for implant removal surgeries and minimize long-term complications. Each type has distinct advantages, catering to various clinical needs in orthopedic procedures.</p></p>
