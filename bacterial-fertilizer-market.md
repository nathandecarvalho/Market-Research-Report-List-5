<p><p>The bacterial fertilizer market comprises several types, primarily nitrogen-fixing and phosphate-solubilizing bacteria, along with other beneficial microorganisms. Nitrogen-fixing bacteria convert atmospheric nitrogen into forms accessible to plants, enhancing soil fertility. Phosphate-solubilizing bacteria convert insoluble phosphorus compounds into soluble forms, promoting plant nutrient uptake. Other microbial types may include potassium solubilizers and beneficial fungi, each contributing to soil health and sustainable agriculture. These fertilizers support crop growth while reducing reliance on chemical fertilizers, promoting eco-friendly farming practices.</p></p>
