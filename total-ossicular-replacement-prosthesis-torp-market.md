<p><p>Total Ossicular Replacement Prosthesis (TORP) is a medical device used in ear surgeries to replace damaged or missing ossicles, the small bones in the middle ear that are crucial for sound transmission. TORP is often utilized in procedures for patients suffering from chronic otitis media, otosclerosis, or other conditions affecting hearing. These prostheses enhance auditory function by ensuring the proper mechanical linkage between the eardrum and the inner ear.</p><p>The TORP market is witnessing significant growth, driven by increasing prevalence of ear disorders, advancements in surgical techniques, and rising awareness about hearing impairment treatment options. Innovations in materials and design have also contributed to improved patient outcomes, further fueling demand.
