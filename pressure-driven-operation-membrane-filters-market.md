<p><p>Pressure-driven operation membrane filters are classified into four main types based on their pore sizes and applications. Microfiltration removes larger particles and microorganisms, typically used for water clarification. Ultrafiltration targets smaller solutes, effective in processing dairy and juice. Nanofiltration allows selective ion and small organic molecule removal, commonly used in water softening. Reverse osmosis has the smallest pore size, effectively desalting water and purifying various liquids. Each type offers unique benefits for diverse industrial and municipal applications.</p></p>
