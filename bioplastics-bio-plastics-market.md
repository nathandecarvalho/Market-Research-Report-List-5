<p><p>Bioplastics are renewable alternatives to conventional plastics, derived from biomass such as plants and microorganisms. The market primarily divides into biodegradable and non-biodegradable bio-based types. Biodegradable bioplastics can decompose naturally, reducing environmental impact, and are ideal for applications like packaging and disposable cutlery. Non-biodegradable bio-based plastics, while derived from renewable resources, do not break down easily and are similar to traditional plastics, making them suitable for durable products like containers and automotive parts. Both types cater to sustainability goals.</p></p>
