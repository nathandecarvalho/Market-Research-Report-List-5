<p><p>Anti-ulcer drugs are medications designed to treat and manage ulcers in the gastrointestinal tract, primarily peptic ulcers, which include gastric and duodenal ulcers. These drugs work by reducing stomach acid production, protecting the stomach lining, or eradicating Helicobacter pylori, a bacteria often responsible for ulcer formation. Common classes of anti-ulcer medications include proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), H2-receptor antagonists, and mucosal protective agents.</p><p>The Anti-ulcer Drug Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of gastrointestinal disorders, lifestyle changes, and rising stress levels among populations. The market is also benefiting from advancements in drug formulations and the introduction of new medications. Additionally, the growing awareness of digestive health and prevention of ulcer complications has escalated demand for these therapeutic agents.</p><p>Market growth is further fueled by the aging population, as older adults are more susceptible to digestive ailments.
