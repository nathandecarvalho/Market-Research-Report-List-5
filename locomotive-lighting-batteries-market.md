<p><p>The locomotive lighting batteries market comprises three primary types: lead-acid, lithium-ion, and nickel-cadmium batteries. Lead-acid batteries are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and reliability, though they are heavier and less efficient. Lithium-ion batteries offer higher energy density, longer life cycles, and reduced weight, making them increasingly popular for modern locomotives. Nickel-cadmium batteries provide good performance in extreme temperatures but have a higher environmental impact and maintenance needs, limiting their widespread adoption in locomotive applications.</p></p>
