<p><p>Coffee roasters come in various types, including Direct-Fire, Semi-Direct Fire with Half Hot Air, and Hot-Air systems. Direct-Fire roasters use flames for heat, offering robust flavor but requiring careful temperature control. Semi-Direct Fire combines direct heat and hot air, providing a balanced roasting environment. Hot-Air roasters rely solely on hot air circulation, ensuring even roasting and minimal charring. Other types may include hybrid or specialty systems that integrate unique technology for specific roasting needs, catering to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
