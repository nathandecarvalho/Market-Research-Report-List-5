<p><p>The Ethernet backhaul equipment market consists of two primary types: wired and wireless. Wired backhaul typically utilizes fiber optic or copper cables to transmit data, offering high-speed, reliable connections, ideal for dense urban environments. In contrast, wireless backhaul employs technologies like microwave or millimeter-wave links, providing flexibility and quicker deployment in areas lacking infrastructure. Both solutions serve to connect remote access points to core networks, ensuring efficient data transfer and supporting the growing demands of mobile and internet services.</p></p>
