<p><p>Mild-hybrid vehicles (MHEVs) are a type of hybrid vehicle that combines a traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) with a small electric motor and battery system. Unlike full hybrids, mild hybrids cannot run solely on electric power but assist the ICE to improve fuel efficiency, reduce emissions, and enhance performance. The electric motor supports functions like start-stop systems, regenerative braking, and torque assistance, making MHEVs a cost-effective and efficient alternative to conventional vehicles.</p><p>The Mild-Hybrid Vehicles Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing environmental regulations, rising fuel prices, and consumer demand for more sustainable transportation options. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12.2% during the forecast period. This growth is fueled by advancements in battery technology, government incentives for eco-friendly vehicles, and the automotive industry's shift toward electrification.</p><p>Key trends in the MHEV market include the integration of 48-volt electrical systems, which provide better energy recovery and efficiency, and the adoption of mild-hybrid technology in a wider range of vehicle segments, including SUVs and luxury cars. Automakers are also focusing on lightweight materials and improved powertrain designs to enhance performance and reduce emissions. As the automotive industry transitions toward electrification, mild hybrids are expected to play a crucial role in bridging the gap between traditional ICE vehicles and fully electric vehicles.</p></p>
