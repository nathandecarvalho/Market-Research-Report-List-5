<p><p>The Clean Label Enzymes market comprises various enzyme types, each serving distinct functions. Carbohydrase breaks down carbohydrates into simpler sugars, commonly used in baking and brewing. Amylase specifically targets starches, enhancing sweetness and texture in food. Cellulase aids in the digestion of cellulose, improving fiber content in products. Protease facilitates protein breakdown, enhancing flavor and digestibility in meat and dairy. Other enzymes include lipases and lactases, supporting diverse applications across food, beverages, and nutraceutical industries, emphasizing clean labeling.</p></p>
