<p><p>Multi-Level Cell (MLC) NAND Flash Memory is categorized into two main market types: 2D and 3D. </p><p>2D NAND stores data in a single layer, leading to limited density and slower performance as cells become densely packed. In contrast, 3D NAND stacks multiple layers of cells vertically, enhancing data storage capacity, speed, and endurance. This technology reduces issues like interference and allows for higher bit density, making 3D NAND the preferred choice for modern storage solutions in smartphones, SSDs, and data centers.</p></p>
